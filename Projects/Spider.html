Sweet Defense

My role: Game engineer


"Spider Collider" is a family-friendly physics-based puzzle game. Play as a spider as it collects flies and avoids obstacles to get back to its mate at home. The game allows the player to attach and detach themselves from connection points spread throughout the levels. With a limited amount of legs, the player has to be careful where they swing and think strategically before they make a move.

About the game

Spider Collider gives the player three types of connections that may be be available throughout the levels, each with their own unique utility that allow the player to overcome obstacles if used correctly.

This game was made in 2 weeks for a team project where the goal was to create a physics-based puzzle game. As one of the two engineers on the team my job was to create the physics engine and write collision code for the game. This code was written in C# with the Saxion GXPEngine.

The game consists of 10 levels, with the beginning few serving the purpose of introducing the connections to the player, one by one. As the player progresses further into the game, more connections are used in conjunction with one another to create a bigger challenge.
